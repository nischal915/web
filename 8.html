Short Answer Questions

1. Challenges in Creating a Digital Society in Nepal

What challenges are we facing to create a digital society in the Nepalese context?

Nepal faces several challenges in creating a digital society, including inadequate digital infrastructure, a significant digital divide between urban and rural areas, limited access to reliable internet, and a lack of skilled manpower and local ownership of digital systems. These challenges hinder the widespread adoption of digital technologies and limit the potential benefits of a digital society. Furthermore, issues like power outages and lack of digital literacy also pose significant barriers.

2. Computer Ethics and Commandments

What is computer ethics? What are the commandments of computer ethics?

Computer ethics refers to moral guidelines governing the responsible use of computers and digital technologies. The commandments of computer ethics, introduced by the Computer Ethics Institute in 1992, include principles like not using a computer to harm others, not interfering with other people's computer work, and respecting the privacy of others. These commandments serve as a foundation for ethical behavior in the digital age, promoting a culture of responsibility and respect among users.

3. Role of Computer Ethics

Discuss about the role of computer ethics in the formation of secure and reliable digital society.

Computer ethics plays a crucial role in forming a secure and reliable digital society by promoting responsible behavior, protecting privacy, and preventing cybercrimes. It ensures that technology is used ethically and safely, fostering trust among users and stakeholders. By adhering to ethical standards, individuals and organizations can mitigate risks associated with digital technologies and contribute to a more secure online environment.

4. Basic Principles of Information Security

Explain the basic principles/components of information security.

The basic principles of information security include confidentiality, integrity, and availability. Confidentiality ensures that data is protected from unauthorized access, integrity ensures that data remains accurate and complete, and availability ensures that data is accessible when needed. These principles form the core of information security practices, guiding efforts to safeguard sensitive information in digital systems.

5. Definition of Cyber Law and Major Fields

Define cyber law. What are the major fields of cyber law?

Cyber law deals with legal issues related to ICT, including internet, computer networks, and electronic transactions. Major fields include digital signatures, copyright law, trademark law, telecommunication law, data protection, and electronic transaction acts. Cyber law provides a legal framework for addressing cybercrimes and ensuring compliance with digital regulations.

6. Signs of Malware Attack

How do you know that your computer has been a victim of malware attack?

Signs of a malware attack include slow performance, frequent crashes, unusual pop-ups, and unauthorized changes to system settings. Additionally, users may notice suspicious network activity, unfamiliar programs running in the background, or unexpected changes to files and folders. Recognizing these signs early can help mitigate the impact of malware and prevent further damage.

7. Protecting Against Cybercrime

How can you protect you and your computer system from cybercrime?

Protection measures include using antivirus software, strong passwords, avoiding suspicious links, keeping software updated, and using a firewall. Regular backups of important data, using secure networks, and being cautious with email attachments also help prevent cyber attacks. Moreover, staying informed about the latest threats and best practices is crucial for maintaining robust cybersecurity.

8. Advantages and Disadvantages of Digital Signature

What are the advantages and disadvantages of digital signature?

Advantages of digital signatures include ensuring authenticity and integrity of documents, being legally binding, and efficient. However, disadvantages include requiring specialized software and hardware, and potential vulnerability to cyber attacks if not properly secured. Additionally, the cost of implementing digital signature systems can be high, and there may be compatibility issues with different systems.

9. Areas Covered by Cyber Law

Explain various areas that are generally covered in cyber law.

Areas covered by cyber law include digital signatures, copyright law, trademark law, telecommunication law, data protection, and electronic transactions. These areas address legal issues arising from digital activities, providing a framework for regulating and enforcing rights in the digital space.

10. Cyber Law in Nepal

Explain cyber law in Nepal with major provisions included.

Nepal's cyber law includes provisions for monetary fines and imprisonment for cyber crimes, digital signatures, and legal hearings through IT tribunals. It was revised in 2063 B.S. and aims to control cybercrimes like hacking and online fraud. The law also covers issues related to data protection and electronic transactions, providing legal recourse for victims of cybercrimes.

11. Objectives of ICT Policy 2072

List out the major objectives of ICT Policy 2072.

The ICT Policy 2072 aims to enhance digital infrastructure, promote e-governance, increase access to ICT services, foster a digital economy in Nepal, support ICT education, and ensure cybersecurity. Additionally, it seeks to encourage digital entrepreneurship, improve digital literacy, develop e-commerce, and expand broadband connectivity across the country.

Long Answer Questions

1. Activities Considered as Cybercrime

Explain at least ten activities that are considered as cybercrime.

  • Hacking: Unauthorized access to computer systems or networks.
  • Phishing: Attempting to obtain sensitive information via deceptive emails or messages.
  • Cyber stalking: Harassing or threatening individuals online.
  • Email bombing: Sending large volumes of emails to overwhelm a recipient's inbox.
  • Online fraud: Using digital platforms to deceive and steal from others.
  • Identity theft: Stealing personal data to impersonate someone else.
  • Cyber bullying: Using digital means to intimidate or harass others.
  • Malware distribution: Spreading malicious software to harm computer systems.
  • Ransomware attacks: Encrypting data and demanding payment for decryption.
  • Unauthorized access: Accessing computer systems without permission.

2. Malicious Software Attacks

What do you mean by malicious software? Explain any ten malicious software attacks.

Malicious software is designed to harm or exploit a computer system. Examples of malicious software attacks include:

  • Viruses: Programs that replicate and spread to other files.
  • Worms: Self-replicating malware that spreads without user interaction.
  • Trojans: Disguised as legitimate software but contain malicious code.
  • Ransomware: Encrypts data and demands ransom for decryption.
  • Spyware: Secretly monitors user activity and collects data.
  • Adware: Displays unwanted advertisements.
  • Rootkits: Conceals malware from system administrators.
  • Keyloggers: Records keystrokes to capture sensitive information.
  • Logic bombs: Triggers malicious actions when specific conditions are met.
  • Botnets: Networks of compromised computers used for malicious activities.

3. Intellectual Property and Legal Provisions in Nepal

Explain intellectual property along with its types in detail. What legal provisions we have in Nepal to preserve intellectual property rights?

Intellectual property includes creations of the mind like inventions, literary works, and designs. Types include patents, copyrights, trademarks, and trade secrets. Nepal has laws to protect IP rights, including the Copyright Act and Patent Act, which provide legal protection against infringement. These laws ensure that creators and innovators receive due recognition and compensation for their work.

4. Digital Signature and Working Mechanism

Explain digital signature along with its working mechanism.

A digital signature is an electronic equivalent of a handwritten signature, ensuring authenticity and integrity of digital documents. It uses a pair of keys: a private key for signing and a public key for verification. This mechanism ensures that documents are tamper-proof and legally binding, facilitating secure electronic transactions and communications.
